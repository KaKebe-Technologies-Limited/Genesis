What We Teach

Our Curriculum

Genesis Primary School follows the Uganda National Curriculum Authority (NCA) syllabus for all classes, ensuring students are well prepared for the Primary Leaving Examination and secondary school entry.

English Language

Reading, writing, comprehension, grammar, and oral communication — strong English is the foundation of everything else.

Mathematics

Number concepts, problem-solving, geometry, measurement, and the practical thinking skills that go far beyond arithmetic.

Science

Biology, physical science, and environmental studies through both theory and hands-on practical work.

Social Studies

History, geography, civics, and responsible citizenship — understanding Uganda and the wider world.

Religious Education

Moral and spiritual development through structured Religious Education that builds character and values.

Creative Arts

Music, drawing, drama — activities that nurture creativity, expression, and cultural appreciation.

Physical Education

Sports and games that develop fitness, motor skills, teamwork, and a healthy relationship with physical activity.

ICT / Computer Studies

Basic computing skills and digital literacy to prepare students for a world that runs on technology.

Class Structure

Lower & Upper Primary

Our school progresses from foundational learning in P1–P4 to examination-focused preparation in P5–P7.

P1 – P4

Lower Primary

The foundation years are where everything begins. We focus on building strong literacy, numeracy, and the study habits that will carry students for the rest of their education. Class sizes are carefully managed so teachers can give real individual attention.

  • Phonics, reading, and basic writing
  • Number foundations and mental arithmetic
  • Introduction to all core subjects
  • Building discipline and classroom culture
  • Termly reports and parent conferences
P5 – P7

Upper Primary

Upper primary intensifies the academic preparation significantly. By P7, students are doing structured revision, mock examinations, and targeted support in weaker subject areas to ensure they are genuinely ready for PLE.

  • Rigorous syllabus coverage for all PLE subjects
  • Mock examinations each term
  • Subject-specific study strategies and time management
  • Remedial sessions for students who need support
  • Career awareness and secondary school guidance
How We Teach

Our Approach to Teaching

01

Interactive Learning

We use participatory methods that get students actively involved — questions, discussions, and exploration are welcomed, not discouraged.

02

Practical Application

Concepts are connected to real-world situations. We help students understand the "why" — not just memorise the "what".

03

Regular Assessment

Ongoing tests and classwork evaluations track progress so we can intervene early and ensure no child falls silently behind.

04

Targeted Support

Students who need extra help receive it. Remedial sessions and individual guidance are part of how we operate — not exceptions.

Beyond the Classroom

Co-Curricular Activities

Whole-person development happens in and out of the classroom. Our activities build teamwork, confidence, creativity, and leadership.

Sports & Athletics

Football, netball, athletics, and more — physical education that develops fitness, discipline, and team spirit.

Music & Cultural Dance

Choir, traditional instruments, and dance — nurturing talent while celebrating Ugandan cultural heritage.

Drama & Public Speaking

Performances and debate that build confidence, communication skills, and the ability to express oneself clearly.

Science & Reading Clubs

Interest groups that go deeper into subjects students love, encouraging curiosity and peer-to-peer learning.

Students participate in school and community projects that develop social responsibility and empathy.

Cultural Days & Events

Annual celebrations of Ugandan culture, history, and identity that build pride and a sense of belonging.
